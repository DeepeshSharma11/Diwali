About Govardhan Puja

The Festival of Mountain Worship

Govardhan Puja is a Hindu festival celebrated on the next day of Diwali. It commemorates the divine act of Lord Krishna lifting the Govardhan Hill to protect the people of Vrindavan from the wrath of Indra, the god of rain.

Significance of Govardhan Puja

This festival holds deep spiritual and cultural significance:

  • Celebrates Lord Krishna's divine leela of lifting Govardhan Hill
  • Symbolizes the importance of nature and environment protection
  • Represents the victory of devotion over ego and pride
  • Emphasizes the importance of community and collective worship
  • Highlights the significance of cow worship in Hindu culture

Govardhan Puja Traditions

Traditional Govardhan Puja celebrations include:

  • Preparing Annakoot (mountain of food) with 56 or 108 varieties
  • Worshipping Govardhan Hill made from cow dung
  • Performing parikrama (circumambulation) around the hill
  • Offering food to Lord Krishna and distributing as prasad
  • Worshipping cows as a symbol of prosperity and nature
  • Listening to the story of Krishna lifting Govardhan Hill
